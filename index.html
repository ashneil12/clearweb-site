Straight answers

What plumbers ask me.

Will it put my customers off?+

It reads like a text from your office, in your business's name. Callers get an answer in seconds instead of a dead line and a beep. In practice people are relieved someone got back to them.

I've already got voicemail.+

Keep it. The gap voicemail can't cover is the callers who hang up without leaving a message, which these days is most of them. The text reaches those people in the minute they'd otherwise ring your competitor.

Do I need a new number or a new phone?+

No. Your number stays exactly as it is and nothing changes for callers. It only wakes up when a call goes unanswered. I check it works with your setup before you pay anything.

What happens with calls it can't handle?+

Anything odd, angry or complicated gets handed straight to you with the caller's number and what they said. It takes down details and books jobs. It doesn't pretend to be a plumber.

Am I tied into a contract?+

No. Month to month, cancel whenever. If it isn't obviously paying for itself in caught jobs, you should cancel.
